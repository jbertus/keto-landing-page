Carb Manager vs Keto Contractor: Tracking Your Carbs vs Deciding Your Meals

Carb Manager is one of the most popular keto and low-carb tracking apps - a big food database, macro logging, barcode scanning, and recipes. If you want to record what you already ate, it does that well.

Where Carb Manager is strong: A mature, polished tracker with a huge food database, wearable integrations, and years of refinement. If manual logging is your thing, it's a strong logger.

Where it leaves a type 2 diabetic on their own: Tracking is the autopsy - it tells you what a meal did after you ate it. It doesn't decide dinner at 6 PM, doesn't read the menu at a restaurant, and isn't built around a type 2 diagnosis.

Common Questions

Is Carb Manager good for type 2 diabetics?

It can log carbs, which helps. But it's a general keto tracker - it doesn't plan your week around your numbers, read restaurant menus, or tell you what to order. Keto Contractor is built specifically for the type 2 use case.

What's the main difference between Carb Manager and Keto Contractor?

Carb Manager records decisions you already made. Keto Contractor makes the decisions with you - planning the week, building the grocery list, and hacking restaurant menus before you order.
