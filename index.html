The Problem

Diabetic meal planning is broken.

Every other app was built for someone else. Diabetics were an afterthought.

MyFitnessPal has no diabetes avatar

Open MFP today. The onboarding goals are: lose weight, gain weight, gain muscle, manage stress, increase steps. "Manage blood sugar" is not on the list. If their product doesn't see you, the experience won't either.

Generic meal plans don't fit real life

Most "diabetic-friendly" plans assume you'll cook a separate breakfast, lunch, and dinner from scratch every day. If you work 50-hour weeks or you got diagnosed last month, that plan was never realistic. Decision fatigue takes over, you eat whatever's fast.

Carb math eats your mental energy

Counting carbs, splitting them across meals, leaving room for fiber, watching evening intake, that's a full-time job. Most newly-diagnosed diabetics are doing it manually, on paper, with no system to lean on.

The Solution

We do the math. You eat the food.

Tell us where you're at. We generate the meals, split the macros, build the shopping list. You decide when and how you cook.

1

Tell us about you

30-second onboarding: type 2 stage, body stats, snack & dessert habits, evening eating patterns. We compute your daily carb cap and split it across meals, automatically, with fiber accounted for.

2

Get your week

AI generates breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks tuned to your personalized macros. Every recipe has full nutrition including fiber. Shopping list builds itself. Walmart prices included.

3

Cook however you cook

Batch prep on a day off. Cook each meal the night before. Mix it up. Your call. The system removes the daily decision, not the act of cooking.
